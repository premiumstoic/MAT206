04

The Metropolis Algorithm

The core of the simulation lies in the decision-making process. For every random flip of an atom's state, we calculate the change in energy, ΔE. If the energy decreases (ΔE < 0), the new state is energetically favorable and accepted immediately.

However, if the energy increases, we don't immediately reject it. To avoid getting stuck in local minima, we accept the change with a probability defined by the Boltzmann factor:

P(accept) = exp( -ΔE / (kBT) )

Where T is temperature and kB is the Boltzmann constant. This introduces "thermal noise" allowing the system to climb out of energy valleys.
